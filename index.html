About us

Cricket that develops the whole player

StrikeZone Cricket League was founded in Johannesburg to give young South African cricketers a structured, competitive, and enjoyable league experience — from the moment they can hold a bat at Under 9, all the way through to Under 18.

We believe the best way to develop a cricketer is to put them in real match situations and track their progress season by season. Every child who plays in StrikeZone leaves with a digital career record, real match experience, and a love for the game.

Most school-going kids play cricket at school, but many still do not get enough real match time to build confidence and improve consistently. StrikeZone fills that gap with regular competitive fixtures and equal opportunities to bat, bowl, and learn in game situations. Cricket also builds life skills beyond the boundary: players learn teamwork, communication, discipline, and leadership while handling both wins and setbacks with maturity.

How we play

Standard T20 cricket rules apply throughout, with one league-specific addition that makes StrikeZone matches unlike anything else your child will play in.

T20 format — all age groups
All StrikeZone matches are 20 overs per side, played across weekends, and use hard ball only. Every child is guaranteed batting and bowling time in each match.
Playing 11 and squad size
Each team fields 11 players per match. Squads can carry up to 15 players. Rotation is encouraged at younger age groups.
Bowling restrictions
Maximum 4 overs per bowler per match across all age groups. The Impact Player bowling extension at U15 and U18 allows one nominated bowler to bowl up to 6 overs.
Points system
Win: 2 points · Tie: 1 point · No result: 1 point · Loss: 0 points. Net Run Rate (NRR) is the tiebreaker for equal-points teams.
Code of conduct
Players and parents are bound by the StrikeZone code of conduct. Dissent toward umpires, sledging, and unsportsmanlike behaviour are not tolerated at any level.
Signature rule
The Impact Player format
1
Second-chance batting: Each team nominates one Impact Player who can bat again if dismissed — turning every wicket into a tactical decision.
2
Extended bowling: The nominated bowler can bowl up to 6 overs — perfect for deploying your best bowler in a tight finish.
3
U15 & U18 only: The rule applies at older age groups, giving younger players time to master the base game first.
